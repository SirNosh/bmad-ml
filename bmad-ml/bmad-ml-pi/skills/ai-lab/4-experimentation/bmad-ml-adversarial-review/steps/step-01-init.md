# Step 01 - Initialize

1. Load results-report.md and experiment-design.md. Understand the claims being made, the evidence supporting them, and the experimental methodology.
2. Identify all explicit claims in the results report: performance claims, comparison claims, causation claims, and scope/generalization claims.
3. Load the evaluation criteria to understand the statistical framework used to support the claims.
4. Load literature-review.md to compare claimed results against the broader literature context.
5. Adopt an adversarial mindset: your role is to find weaknesses, not confirm strengths. Every claim is "guilty until proven innocent."
6. Identify which claims, if invalidated, would change the overall conclusion. These are the highest-priority targets.
7. Check if prior adversarial reviews exist -- if so, verify that previously raised concerns have been adequately addressed.
8. Present the list of claims to be challenged and the review strategy to the user.

9. If operating in autonomous mode (invoked by Nosh), challenge all claims in priority order and skip the confirmation gate.

> Soft gate: "Here are the claims I will challenge and my review approach. Any specific claims or concerns to prioritize?"
