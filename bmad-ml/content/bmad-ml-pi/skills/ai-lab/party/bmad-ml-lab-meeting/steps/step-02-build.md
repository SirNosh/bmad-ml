# Step 02 - Build and Analyze

1. Open the meeting by restating the agenda and format. The moderator (Nosh) sets expectations for focus and brevity.
2. For each agenda item: the responsible agent presents their update, findings, or proposal. Other agents respond with questions, concerns, or supporting evidence.
3. Each agent contributes from their expertise. Research agents (Sova, Sage, Cypher, Viper, Breach, Fade, Astra, Killjoy) focus on scientific rigor, methodology, theory, and evidence. Build agents (Chamber, Jett, Gekko, Omen, KAY/O) focus on architectural decisions, implementation feasibility, code correctness, and statistical validity of claims. If a discussion drifts into product or deployment territory, the moderator should flag it and defer to `bmad-ml-all-hands`.
4. The moderator keeps discussion focused: redirect tangential discussions, ensure quieter agents contribute, and enforce time limits per topic.
5. For each agenda item, drive toward a clear outcome: a decision made, a question answered, an action item assigned, or a topic deferred with a reason.
6. Document decisions as they are made, including the rationale and any dissenting views.
7. Assign action items with owners, deadlines, and acceptance criteria. Each action item should be specific enough that completion is unambiguous.
8. Identify cross-cutting themes: issues that affect multiple experiments, the whole lab's research trajectory, or shared compute infrastructure. Flag these for strategic follow-up.
9. Reserve the final portion for "parking lot" items: topics that were raised but deferred. Decide whether each goes on the next meeting's agenda or becomes a separate workflow.
10. Close with a round-table: each agent states their top priority or concern going forward.

> Soft gate: "All agenda items have been discussed. Any additional topics before I produce the meeting notes?"
