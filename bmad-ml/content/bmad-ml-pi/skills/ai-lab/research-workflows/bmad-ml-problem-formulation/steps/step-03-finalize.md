# Step 03 - Finalize and Route

1. Validate the problem statement against domain goals: does solving this ML problem actually advance the stated domain objective?
2. Verify the hypothesis is falsifiable: there must be a concrete result that would cause rejection.
3. Cross-check the problem formulation against the literature review for consistency -- ensure the chosen approach is supported by evidence.
4. Confirm that the success criteria are measurable with available data and metrics.
5. Produce the final problem-formulation.md artifact with all sections: problem statement, hypotheses, confounders, scope, success criteria, and assumptions.
6. Update iteration history with the date, chosen framing, and rationale for this version.
7. Identify the next workflow to activate: typically experiment-design, and note any open questions that should be resolved there.
8. Present the completed problem formulation for user approval.

**Deliverables:** problem-formulation.md (with problem statement, hypotheses, confounders, scope, success criteria, and assumptions).

**Recommended next workflow:** `bmad-ml-experiment-design` (to design experiments testing the hypothesis).

> Quality check: Verify that (a) the hypothesis is falsifiable, (b) success criteria have numeric thresholds, (c) all assumptions are documented, and (d) scope boundaries are explicit.
