# Step 01 - Initialize

1. Confirm the lab meeting scope: the agenda must be internal to the AI Lab (research, experimentation, architecture, training, analysis). If the topic requires AI Startup input, redirect to `bmad-ml-all-hands`. If the topic is a pure research discussion among the research group, redirect to `bmad-ml-research-party`.
2. Select the meeting format: **Sprint Retrospective** (review what worked and what didn't in the last experiment cycle), **Direction Setting** (decide on research and experiment priorities, compute allocation), **Triage** (assess and prioritize experiment blockers, failed runs, or resource contention), or **Full Debrief** (comprehensive status review across all active Lab work).
3. Determine which AI Lab agents to include based on the agenda topics. Research group: Sova, Sage, Cypher, Viper, Breach, Fade, Astra, Killjoy. Build group: Chamber, Jett, Gekko, Omen, KAY/O. Select only those whose expertise is relevant.
4. Frame the agenda: list 3-5 specific topics or questions to address. Each topic should have a clear decision or outcome expected.
5. Gather relevant status artifacts: experiment-status.yaml, recent results-report.md, readiness-check.md, code-review findings, adversarial-review findings, and any blockers or issues to discuss.
6. Assign a moderator role (typically Nosh, acting in Lab Director mode) to keep the meeting focused and time-boxed.
7. Define the expected meeting output: decisions made, action items assigned, and next steps documented.
8. Set time expectations: each agenda item should have an estimated discussion length to prevent any single topic from dominating.
9. Present the agenda and participant list to the user for approval.

10. If operating in autonomous mode (invoked by Nosh), use the directive to set the agenda and skip the confirmation gate.

> Soft gate: "Here is the AI Lab meeting agenda, format, and participants. Ready to convene, or any agenda changes?"
