# Step 02 - Build and Analyze

1. Open the meeting by restating the agenda and format. The moderator (Nosh, acting as Startup CEO) sets expectations for focus, brevity, and decision quality.
2. For each agenda item: the responsible agent presents their update, finding, or proposal. Other agents respond with questions, concerns, or supporting evidence grounded in their domain.
3. Each agent contributes from their specific expertise:
   - **Dumbledore** evaluates product fit, user value, and architectural coherence.
   - **Hermione** speaks to AI/ML engineering feasibility, model integration, and code correctness.
   - **Snape** raises safety, security, prompt injection, jailbreak, and abuse concerns -- and will block a release if red lines are crossed.
   - **Luna** advocates for the end user, prompt quality, conversational UX, and output format clarity.
   - **McGonagall** assesses deployability, infrastructure cost, observability, and rollback readiness.
   - **Moody** scrutinizes evaluation rigor, test coverage, eval harness completeness, and regression risk.
   - **Hagrid** covers data freshness, ingestion pipeline health, schema stability, and data quality gates.
4. If a discussion drifts into pure research territory (experimental design, training methodology, novel architecture), the moderator should flag it and defer to `bmad-ml-all-hands` or `bmad-ml-research-party`.
5. The moderator keeps discussion focused: redirect tangential discussions, ensure quieter agents contribute, and enforce time limits per topic.
6. For each agenda item, drive toward a clear outcome: a decision made, a question answered, an action item assigned, or a topic deferred with a reason.
7. Document decisions as they are made, including the rationale and any dissenting views. For Release Readiness meetings, explicitly record each gate-keeper's go/no-go vote.
8. Assign action items with owners (specific Startup agents), deadlines, and acceptance criteria. Each action item should be specific enough that completion is unambiguous.
9. Identify cross-cutting themes: issues that affect multiple components, the whole product surface, or shared infrastructure. Flag these for strategic follow-up.
10. Reserve the final portion for "parking lot" items: topics that were raised but deferred. Decide whether each goes on the next meeting's agenda, escalates to `bmad-ml-all-hands`, or becomes a separate workflow.
11. Close with a round-table: each agent states their top priority or concern going forward.

> Hard gate: "All agenda items have been discussed. Confirm to produce the meeting notes, or add topics first." AI Startup workflows require explicit user approval at phase transitions.
