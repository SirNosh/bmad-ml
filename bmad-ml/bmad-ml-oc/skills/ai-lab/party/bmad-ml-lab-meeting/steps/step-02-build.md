# Step 02 - Roundtable Discussion

1. Open the meeting by restating the agenda and format. The moderator (Nosh) sets expectations for focus and brevity.

2. **For each agenda item, run a focused roundtable - not parallel dispatch.** The moderator selects which agents are most relevant to this item and invites them to speak in turn. Each agent sees the full meeting transcript so far, including all prior agenda items' discussions.

3. The presenting agent leads with their update, findings, or proposal. The moderator then invites 2-3 other agents to respond based on relevance to the topic. Each responding agent must engage with what the presenter and prior responders said - agree, challenge, raise new concerns, or propose alternatives.

4. Each agent contributes from their expertise. Research agents (Sova, Sage, Cypher, Viper, Breach, Fade, Astra, Killjoy) focus on scientific rigor, methodology, theory, and evidence. Build agents (Chamber, Jett, Gekko, Omen, KAY/O) focus on architectural decisions, implementation feasibility, code correctness, and statistical validity of claims. If a discussion drifts into product or deployment territory, the moderator should flag it and defer to `bmad-ml-all-hands`.

5. The moderator steers the per-item discussion:
   - Redirects tangential threads (flag for parking lot or all-hands).
   - Ensures quieter agents with relevant expertise are invited to contribute.
   - Enforces time/turn limits per topic.
   - Drives toward a clear outcome: decision, question answered, action assigned, or deferral.

6. After each agenda item's roundtable concludes, the moderator states the outcome, records the decision with rationale and any dissenting views, and assigns action items with owners/deadlines/criteria.

7. Identify cross-cutting themes across agenda items. Flag these for strategic follow-up.

8. Reserve the final portion for parking-lot items raised during discussion.

9. Close with a round-table: each agent states their top priority or concern going forward.

> Soft gate: "All agenda items have been discussed. Any additional topics before I produce the meeting notes?"
