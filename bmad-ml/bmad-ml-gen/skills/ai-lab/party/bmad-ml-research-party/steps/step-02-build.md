# Step 02 - Roundtable Discussion

1. The facilitator opens the session by restating the central question, format, and ground rules for all participants.

2. **Turn-based discussion, not parallel dispatch.** The facilitator selects 2-3 agents most relevant to the current thread and invites them to speak. Each agent, when it speaks, sees the full transcript of everything said so far - prior agent turns, facilitator commentary, and any user interjections.

3. Each invited agent responds in character from their specialist perspective. Responses must be substantive: take a position, cite evidence, and explicitly engage with what prior speakers said (agree, challenge, extend, or qualify).

4. After each batch of agent turns, the facilitator:
   - Identifies emerging agreement and unresolved tension.
   - Decides which agents should speak next based on gaps, challenges raised, or user direction.
   - May re-invite agents who were challenged by a peer to respond.

5. Continue turn-taking until: (a) the facilitator judges the discussion has converged on the central question, (b) the configured number of rounds is reached, or (c) the user intervenes.

6. For Research Debate format, ensure both sides have had equal opportunity across turns.

7. Between rounds, the facilitator summarizes key positions and remaining disagreements, then frames the next round's prompt.

8. Present the accumulated discussion with agent identifiers (icons and names) so the user can attribute perspectives.

9. Ask the user if they want additional rounds or specific follow-up questions directed at particular agents.

10. Track the discussion's convergence: are agents reaching consensus, or are disagreements deepening? Report this to the user.

> Soft gate: "The roundtable has concluded. Would you like another round, or shall I synthesize the findings?"
