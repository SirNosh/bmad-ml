# Step 01 - Initialize

1. Confirm the startup meeting scope: the agenda must be internal to the AI Startup division (product design, AI/ML engineering, safety sign-off, prompt/UX, MLOps/deployment, QA/evaluation, data pipelines). If the topic requires AI Lab research or experimental evidence, redirect to `bmad-ml-all-hands`.
2. Select the meeting format: **Sprint Review** (walk through the current sprint's product deliverables, demos, open risks), **Sprint Retrospective** (review what worked and what didn't in the last startup sprint; surface process improvements), **Design Review** (review an architecture, RAG design, agent system, or prompt strategy with cross-functional feedback), **Release Readiness** (gate a release -- Moody signs off on quality, Snape on safety, McGonagall on deployment, Dumbledore on product fit), **Triage** (rapid assessment of production incidents, user-reported bugs, or safety issues), or **Direction Setting** (align on product direction, feature priorities, and risk acceptance).
3. Determine which AI Startup agents to include based on the agenda topics. Available: Dumbledore (AI Product Architect), Hermione (AI/ML Engineering), Snape (AI Security & Safety), Luna (Prompt Engineering & AI UX), McGonagall (MLOps & Deployment), Moody (AI QA & Evaluation), Hagrid (Data Pipeline & Integration). For a Release Readiness gate, include Moody, Snape, McGonagall, and Dumbledore at minimum. For a Design Review, the presenting domain's lead plus at least two reviewers from adjacent domains.
4. Frame the agenda: list 3-5 specific topics or questions to address. Each topic should have a clear decision or outcome expected.
5. Gather relevant status artifacts: product-brief.md, architecture.md, prompt-library entries, deployment-runbook.md, eval reports, incident reports, recent user feedback, and any blockers to discuss.
6. Assign the moderator role to Nosh, acting as Startup CEO, to keep the meeting focused and time-boxed.
7. Define the expected meeting output: decisions made, action items assigned with owners and deadlines, risks acknowledged, and next steps documented.
8. Set time expectations: each agenda item should have an estimated discussion length to prevent any single topic from dominating.
9. Present the agenda and participant list to the user for approval.

> Hard gate: "Here is the AI Startup meeting agenda, format, and participants. Confirm to convene, or specify changes." AI Startup workflows require explicit user approval at phase transitions -- do not bypass this gate.
